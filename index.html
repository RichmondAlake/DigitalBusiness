    SAP



    SAP SE (Systems, Applications & Products in Data Prorcessing) has an enterprise application called SAP AR Warehouse Picker mobile app is an app that works with smart glasses that enables warehouse pickers to work more efficiently and reducing the amount of time workers use handhelds to scan items. The app guides warehouse workers through the tasks they need to do to pick up the items. It uses voice recognition to navigate through the interface and data entry. The app and smart glasses work in tandem to provide prompts for the tasks that need to be complete. With the smart glass, the software is able to scan QR codes, making the process of picking items quicker.

    SAP AR Service Technician mobile is an app that works similar to the Warehouse Picker app but it allows for technicians to get step-by-step instructions on completing tasks they have been called out to and also provide 3D model representation of parts. It also allows for the technician to call other technicians in the expertise of the task and get over the shoulder assistance.

    Scope

    Scope Augmented Reality Training System is a wearable eyewear glasses that provides a user guided training solution on industrial components. It gives the ability for a untrained user to maintain a component by providing them with step by step instructions and animated directions with diagrams. Using the built in camera, it uses optical recognition to identify parts of a component and highlight and animate them to visualise how to perform maintenance on parts.

    MeView



    MeView is an augmented reality application mobile application from Mitsubishi Electric and Metaio. Similar to the Scope Augmented Reality Training System, it gives step-by-step instructions on how to undertake maintenance on its systems and units. Both of these developments replace the traditional manuals in favour for a virtual interactive manual.

    NGRAIN



    NGRAIN is augmented reality application developers who have developed solutions for industrial enterprises and education. Some of their clients who use their systems are Lockheed Martin, British Columbia Institute of Technology and the US Army. Lockheed Martin is an fighter jet aircraft manufacture who use NGRAIN’s AR technology in there damage assessment and repair system to maintain and repair their F-35 and F-22 aircrafts. They use Epson Moverio BT-200 glasses, which features a camera that uses motion and depth sensors to virtually display images onto a technician’s workspace. It shows how to assemble components of an aircraft using instructions optical recognition of parts to show exactly how parts fit together.

    NGRAIN launched in 2001 and decided to cater to the Canadian military market, developing 3D visualisation that gave a new way to train using interactive models to assemble and dis-assemble models. The success of this got them international recognition. Feedback from their clients showed that the 3D interactive training improved first-time right by over 30%, training throughput by up-to 60%, knowledge retention for specific tasks by 20-30%. This technology is used today in the US military and UK Ministry of Defence. They have since developed other applications that allow technicians to perform maintenance of their equipment in the aerospace, defence, energy and medical industries across the globe (Asia, Europe and North America). In 2013, NGRAIN received a $9.5 million contribution from the Federal Strategic Aerospace and Defence Initiative to fund research and development into advanced 3D technology due to their early success.
